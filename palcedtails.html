Mysore palcce

Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is a historical palace located in Mysore, Karnataka, India. It served as the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty for over 600 years. The palace is renowned for its stunning Indo-Saracenic archi-tecture, blending Hindu, Islamic, Rajput, and Gothic styles. The palace grounds include various structures like the Gombe Thotti (Dolls Pavilion), Kalyana Mantapa (wedding hall), and the Public Durbar Hall.

Architecture:

The palace is a three-story structure, primarily built of gray granite, with a five-story tower topped by a gilded dome. It incorporates elements from different architectural styles, including arches, domes, balconies, and a central tower.

History:

The original palace was destroyed by fire in 1897 and subsequently rebuilt. The current structure was completed in 1912.

Layout and Structures:

The palace grounds include several notable areas, such as:
Gombe Thotti (Dolls Pavilion):
A pavilion that traditionally displayed dolls during the Dasara festivities.
Kalyana Mantapa (Wedding Hall):
A hall used for royal weddings and other ceremonies.
Public Durbar Hall:
A large hall used for public audiences.
Golden Howdah:
Located in the Gombe Thotti, it is used during Dasara processions.

Royal Treasures:

The palace houses a museum displaying royal artifacts, including the Golden Howdah, royal thrones, crowns, and various pieces of art.

Dasara Celebrations:

The palace is a central location for the Mysore Dasara festivities, a grand ten-day celebration.

Timings and Entry:

The palace is open to the public from 10:00 AM to 5:30 PM daily.
The entry fee is ₹70 for adults and ₹30 for children (10-18 years).

Illumination:

The palace is illuminated on Sundays and public holidays from 7:00 PM to 8:00 PM,
creating a spectacular sight.

Sound and Light Show:

A 45-minute sound and light show is presented on weekdays at 7:00 PM & 8:00 PM,
in Kannada (Monday-Wednesday) and English (Thursday-Saturday).
