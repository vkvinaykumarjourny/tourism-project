Karanji Lake

Karanji Lake, also known as Fountain Lake, is a large urban lake in Mysore, India, known for its bird sanctuary and recreational activities. It's located at the foot of Chamundi Hills, adja-cent to the Mysore Zoo, and spans 90 acres. The lake is a popular des-tination for nature lovers, bird watchers, and families, offering a variety of attractions and activities.

Bird Sanctuary:

Karanji Lake is home to over 90 species of resident and migratory birds, including painted storks, pelicans, and darters.

Aviary:

The lake features India's largest walk-through aviary, where visitors can observe various bird species up close.

Butterfly Park:

A butterfly park within the lake area provides a habitat for a variety of butterfly species.

Boating:

Visitors can enjoy boating on the lake, with options for both rowboats and pedal boats.

Watchtower:

A lookout tower offers panoramic views of the lake and surrounding area.

Nature Education Center:

The lake serves as a center for nature education, with facilities for visitors to learn about the local ecosystem.

Walking and Picnics:

The park around the lake provides ample space for walking, relaxing, and enjoying picnics.

History and Management:

The lake was originally built by the King of Mysore as a percolation tank. In 1976, it was taken over by the Mysore Zoo and has since been developed and maintained by them.
